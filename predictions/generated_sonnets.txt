Poor soul, the center of my sinful earth,
Pressed with these rebel powers that thee array,
Why dost thou pine within and suffer dearth,
The very secret of thine eyes,
It is thine purpose to mourn thy lost life.


It is a proud boast that I have left thee,

When thou hast seen me, to tell thee the whole story of thy father's death.

Thou hast long since seen all thy father's sins;

My love is as a fever, longing still
For that which longer nurseth the disease,
Feeding on that which doth preserve the ill,

Mighty is the hundred-three that spendth in purifying the sick

Of love and fear.

(And a large number of them lay down their arms.)

(And they kneel before the Lord, the Lord's beloved.)
