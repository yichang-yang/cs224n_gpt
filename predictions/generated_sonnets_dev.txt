Lo, as a careful huswife runs to catch
One of her feathered creatures broke away,
Sets down her babe, and makes all swift dispatch
To the wild bird which for her aid doth fly.
But since thou art so watchful, that which thou catchest
May by accident convert into sweet gold,
And to thyself shalt afterwards give many precious things,
Thyself being the treasure, many a widow to live.
Then were not thy bounteous deeds so great,
But, being once counted, they should afterwards be counted
And made of many, many a mournful o'erscore.
  But, Lord, be thou thus: love, like a mother holds back
  The wailing babe, which her sweet protecting breast breeds.
  Then, if it live many a day, it liveth oft more;
  Or if it die, it thrice more abundantly.
  Then was the babe made to mourn, which hence it still cries;
  But, being made more, it sings more sweetly.
